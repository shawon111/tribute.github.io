Retirement

Though Mortaza has not retired from Test cricket, he was forced to quit playing Test matches after he injured his right leg while captaining the side for the first time in 2009 against West Indies. Since then, he has not represented his country in any test matches. Considering his injuries and several surgeries, BCB has not allowed him to play in the longer format.[176] On 4 April 2017, Mortaza announced his retirement from Twenty20 internationals after the tour of Sri Lanaka. [177] He played his last T20I on 6 April 2017 against Sri Lanka at the R. Premadasa Stadium.[178] In March 2020, when Zimbabwe toured Bangladesh, ahead of the tour, the BCB announced that it would be Mortaza's last series as captain of the ODI team.[179] Prior to the third ODI, Mortaza confirmed that he would be stepping down as Bangladesh's ODI captain after the series.

Political career

Although he had never been involved in politics during his cricket career, Mortaza collected a parliamentary candidature form for 2018 Bangladeshi general election on 11 November 2018 under Awami League's banner. [180] He contested in the election from Narail-2 constituency and after the declaration of results he eventually won the seat by a huge margin by 2,71,210 votes from his rival. In February 2019, he has become member of the Parliamentary Standing Committee on Youth and Sports Ministry of the eleventh Jatiya Sangsad
